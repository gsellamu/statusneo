# THE 90-SECOND PITCH + BLUEPRINT WALKTHROUGH
**The single most important 90 seconds of the meeting.** When David says *"Tell me about you for this FDE role"* — this is what you say. Then you walk the blueprint.

> **Delivery rule:** This is spoken, not recited. Know the *beats*, not the words. Warm, humble, certain. Echo THEIR language ("contextual intelligence," "modularize for reuse," "vendor-neutral," "fragmentation and waste"). End by handing them the floor.

---

## 🎤 THE 90-SECOND PITCH (say this when asked "tell me about you")

> **[WHO I AM — 15s]**
> "I'm a forward-deployed engineer who builds AI platforms that *ship* — I've taken agentic, real-time systems from whiteboard to production, including a clinical product with live biometric-driven avatars on Unreal. But the reason I'm here isn't my résumé — it's that I didn't study the job description. **I studied your platform.**

> **[WHAT YOU'VE BUILT — 20s] *(echo their mission, credit them)***
> "Modutecture has already done the hard, visionary part: you've built the **digital twin as the single source of truth**, the **rule-bearing Moducules**, the reusable catalog, and the Unity experience. You're solving a *massive* data problem for serial owners and builders — eliminating fragmentation and waste by **modularizing customer data for continuous reuse**, vendor-neutral, delivered through gamification and visual wayfinding. That bet is *right*: buildings should compound, not restart.

> **[THE CRITICAL BLOCKER — 20s] *(the insight that proves you read their system)***
> "But here's what's keeping you from production with *paying* customers: **the product lives between the tools, and there's nothing there yet.** Your own docs show collision as a *concept* — because validation has no home. It can't be authoritative in the Unity client, and nothing server-side computes it. So the Designer → Space Bot → Room → Floor chain *stalls* exactly where correctness should live. **That's the gap between a beautiful demo and a platform an owner will pay for and stake a hospital on.**

> **[THE RESCUE — 20s] *(net-new, never overclaiming)***
> "So I'd add the one layer your roadmap doesn't show yet: a **governed agentic-AI layer** and an **inline deterministic correctness gate** — the AI *proposes*, a deterministic gate *disposes*, and the twin *remembers*. Grounded by a knowledge graph so it cites real codes, never hallucinates. That's what turns your twin from a system that *stores* truth into one that *reasons* and stays **correct-by-construction**. And I didn't just propose it — **I built a working model of it, running on your stack, and I'd like to show you.**

> **[THE FAST-FOLLOW AMBITION — 15s] *(the #1-in-field vision)***
> "Then we earn the rest, one metric at a time — FAANG-grade scale and Autodesk-grade geometry, sequenced, never premature. The goal isn't to catch up. It's to make Modutecture the **#1 Contextual Intelligence Construction platform in AEC** — the one everyone else benchmarks against. **Where would you like me to start — the working model, or the gaps I think I see?**"

> *Then STOP. Hand them the floor.*

## 🧭 THE BLUEPRINT WALKTHROUGH (after the pitch — "let me show you the playbook")

Open **`/showcase.html`** or **`/interview.html`** on screen and narrate the arc. This is the
"here's how I think" map — five movements.

### Movement 1 — "Here's what's yours, and what I'd add" *(honesty first = credibility)*
> "Before anything: I'm not proposing to rebuild what you have. The twin, the Moducules, the catalog, Unity — those are **yours**, and they're good. Three columns: what you've built, what StatusNeo's pod is hardening, and the one net-new layer I'd own — **governed agentic AI + the inline gate + the knowledge graph**. Verified absent from your onboarding docs. That's the opening, and it's where my team's DNA actually is."

### Movement 2 — "The thesis, in one line" *(the architecture spine)*
> "Everything I built rests on one idea: **the twin holds the truth, the renderer owns nothing, the AI proposes, and a deterministic gate disposes.** Unity becomes the GPU. The twin holds the truth. The edge carries the bytes. The brain stays where the data is. Let me show it running."

### Movement 3 — "The working proof" *(switch to the live demo)*
> "Watch a legal placement commit, an illegal one get **rejected with a cited rule** — leaving no trace — and a grounded agent **propose** a bed that the gate **validates** before a human approves it. That's the whole thesis in three clicks: correct-by-construction, grounded, governed. 39 tests, both stacks green, gate runs in 15 microseconds."

### Movement 4 — "How it becomes #1" *(the fast-follow, earned)*
> "Then we scale like the best in the world — but only when a metric demands it. **Geometry like Netflix** (content-addressed at the edge). **State like Meta's feed** (regional working-sets, invalidate-not-fan-out). **AI like edge inference** (co-located, never on the frame path). Autodesk-grade geometry precision is a *triggered* decision, not day-one. Every tier is an ADR with a trigger and a revisit date. That discipline — knowing what **not** to build yet — is how we get to #1 without melting under our own ambition."

### Movement 5 — "The 60-day proof, and your judgment" *(close on partnership)*
> "Concretely: in 60 days, Designer-to-Floor runs end-to-end in production on **one flagship room type** with a real design partner. And two decisions I'd want **your** eyes on first — the geometry-kernel strategy and the tenant-isolation tier — because they're the hardest to retrofit and the decision rights are yours. **That's the playbook: your vision, my disciplined execution, measured proof. Where do you want to go deep?"**

## 🎯 THE THREE THINGS THIS PITCH PROVES (why it works)

1. **You understand THEIR business** — you quoted their mission back, credited their build, named *paying customers* as the goal. Not a generic engineer.
2. **You found the real blocker** — "validation has no home" is a systems insight from reading their docs, not a buzzword. It reframes you from candidate to diagnostician.
3. **You're honest about scope** — "here's what's yours, here's the one layer I'd add" disarms the Lead Architect's #1 fear (a consultant who oversells). Honesty *is* the senior signal.

## ⚠️ WHAT NOT TO SAY (the overclaim traps)

- ❌ "I propose the digital twin" → they HAVE it. Say "your twin."
- ❌ "This is production-ready" → it's a working vertical slice. Say so.
- ❌ "The AI decides" → the AI *proposes*; the **gate** decides. Never blur this in healthcare.
- ❌ "You don't have X" → say "your docs don't show X yet; is that right?" — curiosity, not correction.
- ❌ Naming a number on geometry/scale you haven't measured → "I'd measure before I decide."

## 🧠 MINDSET FOR THE 90 SECONDS
- You're not asking for a job. You're showing a CTO you already see his platform more clearly than most of his team.
- Humble about *their* build, certain about *your* diagnosis.
- The pitch earns you the demo. The demo earns you the contract. **Don't rush the pitch to get to the demo — the pitch IS why they'll watch the demo.**
- Land the last line — *"Where would you like me to start?"* — and **stop talking.**
