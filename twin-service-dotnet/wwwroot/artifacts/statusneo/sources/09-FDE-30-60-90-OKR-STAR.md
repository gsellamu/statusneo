# The 30 / 60 / 90 in OKR & STAR Format
## FDE Practice Activation — Addendum 09
*Objectives and Key Results for execution · STAR narratives for the board and the room · June 2026*

**What this adds.** The charter's Section 4 already lays out a 30/60/90 plan as themes, tasks, and exit criteria, and Sections 5 and 6.3 define the metrics and KPIs. This addendum re-expresses that same plan in two canonical formats it did not yet carry: **OKRs** (an Objective with measurable Key Results, for planning and tracking) and **STAR narratives** (Situation – Task – Action – Result, for interviews, board updates, and case studies). No new claims are introduced — only new structure over the existing plan.

| Format | What it is for | Read it as |
|---|---|---|
| **OKR** | Aligning and tracking execution. An Objective is qualitative and time-bound; Key Results are measurable outcomes (not tasks), scored 0.0–1.0, where ~0.7 is "good." | The operating contract for each horizon. |
| **STAR** | Telling the story. Situation → Task → Action → Result — the structure interviewers, boards, and case studies expect. | The narrative behind each horizon. |

> **Honesty flag (carried from the suite).** The three horizon STARs are **forward-looking** — the plan rendered as narrative, not work already completed. The one retrospective STAR is the Modutecture pilot, which remains framed as a **validated demonstration, not a closed-and-scaled account.** Targets in the Key Results are the charter's stated targets, pending verification where they originate from June-2026 source briefings.

*The 30/60/90 as three Objectives, each with measurable Key Results*

**How to score these.** At each horizon close, score every Key Result 0.0–1.0 (fraction of target achieved). The horizon's score is the mean of its KRs. Treat ~0.7 as on-track, not failure; a steady 1.0 means the targets were set too low. Binary KRs (signed / published / live) score 0 or 1. The Objective itself is not scored — only its Key Results.

# Part B — The STAR Narratives
*The same 30/60/90 as story — plus the Modutecture pilot that proves it*

OKRs are how the practice is tracked; STAR is how it is told — in an interview, a board update, or a reference case. The three horizon narratives below are the plan as a forward-looking story; the fourth is the live proof the plan is grounded in.

### Horizon STAR — Days 0–30: Land & Charter
- **Situation:** StatusNeo has world-class frameworks (the OS Fabric™, the AuthenticAI™ Loop) and deep offshore scale, but no named onshore embedded-delivery motion — and the live Modutecture engagement, though the CTO is enthusiastic, is still informal.
- **Task:** Convert that live momentum into a chartered practice with a referenceable pilot and a qualified target pipeline — in 30 days.
- **Action:** Ran leadership immersion; audited active accounts for the 3–5 where an embedded FDE would have prevented churn or unlocked expansion; formalized Modutecture as the first reference; authored FDE Charter v1 (definition, FDE↔GCC model, FDE Loop™ v1, baselined metrics).
- **Result:** Charter signed; Modutecture referenceable as the pilot; 3–5 targets identified and scored; Time-to-First-Value baseline set on a sub-14-day trajectory.

### Horizon STAR — Days 31–60: Codify & Prove
- **Situation:** One success (Modutecture) proves the embedded motion works, but it is a single data point — not yet repeatable, staffed, or shown to transfer.
- **Task:** Make the motion repeatable and prove it transfers to a second account, with a staffed FDE cadre behind it.
- **Action:** Published the FDE Loop™ Cookbook and the Landing Runbook; identified and began onboarding 2–3 FDEs, each paired with an offshore pod; applied the FDE Loop™ to land a second account; coached client engineers to author their own Gherkin specs.
- **Result:** Methodology documented and teachable; 2–3 FDEs onboarding; second account landed; Client-Authored Spec Ratio > 60%, Agentic PR Merge Rate > 90%, Human Intervention Rate < 2 / 1k LOC.

### Horizon STAR — Days 61–90: Productize & Scale
- **Situation:** The motion is proven and repeatable across two accounts, but it is not yet a named, sellable practice line with its own pipeline.
- **Task:** Institutionalize FDE as a productized, co-sold practice with a pipeline, a health dashboard, and regional enablement.
- **Action:** Launched "StatusNeo FDE" on the site and in the sales motion; stood up the practice dashboard (accounts, expansion revenue, offshore-pull ratio, time-to-trust, NPS); enabled regional co-sell across West Coast, East Coast, EMEA, APAC; published the thought-leadership whitepaper extending the 2026 trilogy.
- **Result:** Named practice live with a pipeline; dashboard reporting to the sponsor; 2–4 reference accounts; all regions enabled; System Adoption Velocity > 75%; Platform Contribution Rate ≥ 2 / pilot.

### Anchor STAR — the Modutecture pilot (the live proof)
*The one retrospective narrative — the engagement that makes the whole charter credible. Framed honestly as a validated demonstration.*
- **Situation:** Modutecture — a StatusNeo customer in the healthcare-AEC space — had compelling but siloed prototypes and no production-grade path: validation "had no home," and the regulated, air-gapped context raised the bar for any solution.
- **Task:** Prove that an embedded FDE plus governed agentic AI could land a production-grade, customer-validated slice on the customer's own stack and de-risk the path to scale — without overclaiming.
- **Action:** Diagnosed the load-bearing gap live and validated it with the customer; architected the contextual-construction twin approach; built the thinnest end-to-end vertical slice on their actual stack; enforced the deterministic correctness gate (model proposes, gate disposes); labeled every deliverable built / proven / next.
- **Result:** A working, customer-validated demonstration on the customer's stack; the CTO was enthusiastic and invited a deeper engagement; the work became the pilot and reference case for the entire FDE practice — explicitly a validated demonstration, not a closed-and-scaled account.
