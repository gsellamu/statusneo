Objection handling (for you — crisp answers to likely pushback)

If the Founder says…You answer
"Onshore kills our margin."FDE doesn't replace offshore — it multiplies it. One FDE opens and de-risks a regulated account a 10–20-person pod then scales. The metric is the offshore-pull ratio: seats opened per FDE landing. It's a revenue-and-access play, not a delivery-cost play.
"Is the Modutecture thing actually real?"Be precise and honest: it's a validated demonstration of the motion, not a signed-and-scaled account. True: the CTO saw the embedded motion work and invited expansion. Not yet true: a closed long-term deal. Converting it is part of the first 30 days — and labeling it honestly is exactly the discipline the practice sells.
"Why him, not a cheaper generic hire?"A generic hire can't walk into a CISO's room in a regulated account. 28 years embedded across Qualcomm, GE Healthcare, Amazon — plus a live proof at our own customer. The role's whole value is onshore architecture authority + earned trust; that's not a junior seat.
"Why now?"Three forces converge: the market is saturated with agent-washing (landed outcomes differentiate); our air-gapped posture already targets buyers who won't go pure-offshore; and we have a live proof in hand. The window is open now.
"What's the risk?"Three honest failure modes — the role gets diluted into generic delivery; we pick the wrong (non-regulated) pilot accounts; or the offshore-pull never materializes. All three are de-risked in the first 30 days: protect the role definition, select the right pilots, instrument the pull-ratio from day one.
"How do we know he can drive AI adoption, not just deploy it?"He measures it, not asserts it — TTFV < 14 days, client-authored specs > 60%, agentic PR-merge > 90%, on a live C-suite dashboard. The transformation has landed when the client stops micromanaging the code and starts authoring the specs.
"How does this map to the role we posted?"The posted role's stated core is the AI Maturity Scan — structured interviews, maturity scoring, capability heatmaps, and calling out where AI should not be used. That is the Clarity / Diagnose phase of the FDE motion. Jeeth runs the scan and ships the fix behind it, at charter level — not an assessor who leaves a deck.
